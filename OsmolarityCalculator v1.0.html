💡 Tips
  • Where's insulin, heparin, or famotidine? These are of negligible osmolarity and can be overlooked
  • Most institutions limit the osmolarity of peripherally-infused solutions to 900 mOsm/L
  • Osmolarity of certain solutions given via short-term infusion are often overlooked despite being beyond the conventional 900 mOsm/L limits, such as N-acetylcysteine, arginine hydrochloride, and sodium bicarbonate
  • Some infusions in pediatric patients of osmolarity >1000 have been recently evaluated here, challenging normal conventional osmolarity cut-offs
