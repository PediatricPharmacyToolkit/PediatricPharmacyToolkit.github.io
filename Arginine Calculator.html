💡 Tips
🔴 Safety warning: This site does not perform cumulative dose checking or infusion rate checking. Clinical judgment and institutional guidance must be applied. 🟠 For most patients, the calculated dose will fall very close to a replacement dose of approximately 1 mEq/kg of arginine hydrochloride. 🟡 Arginine hydrochloride is a vesicant. The risk of tissue injury with extravasation is notable; central routes of administration should be preferred when feasible (pH ~5.6; ~950 mOsm/L). 🟢 Each 1 g of arginine hydrochloride provides approximately 4.75 mEq of chloride. 🔵 If administered orally, doses are often an intolerably large volume and contain high chloride content. Administration by slow enteral infusion via feeding tube may improve tolerability and reduce nausea. 🟣 Your dose frequency is purely expert opinion. Your laboratory monitoring schedule will determine a sensible frequency for the patient.
