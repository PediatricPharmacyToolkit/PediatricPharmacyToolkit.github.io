💡 Tips

  • Aim to lower serum sodium gradually — generally no more than ~10–12 mEq/L in 24 hours (≈0.5 mEq/L/hr). Adjust per clinical context.
  • Faster correction may be indicated only for acute symptomatic cases under specialist guidance.
  • Use conservative targets in neonates and small infants; individualize with clinician/pharmacist input.
  • Caution: Always double-check weight, entered serum sodium, target sodium, TBW coefficient, fluid concentration, and correction time before acting.
Source: Adrogué HJ & Madias NE, N Engl J Med. 2000
